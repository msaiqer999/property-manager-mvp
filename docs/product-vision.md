# Product Vision

## Property Manager

Property Manager is a subscription-based property operations platform for landlords, family property owners, and later professional property management companies, enterprises, and service providers.

The product is focused on day-to-day rental operations: properties, units, tenant records, contracts, rent payments, overdue payments, expenses, documents, reports, activity logs, team roles, maintenance readiness, services, and future data intelligence.

## What This Product Is

- A property operations system.
- A rental management system.
- A landlord workspace.
- A team-based property administration tool.
- A foundation for future maintenance, cleaning, property marketing, short-stay rental operations, mobile applications, SaaS subscriptions, and anonymized market intelligence.

## What This Product Is Not

- It is not a real estate sales platform.
- It is not an open classifieds website.
- It is not an open broker marketplace.
- It is not a public listing platform where anyone can post any property.
- It is not a tenant-facing portal in Stage 1.
- It is not a daily rental booking engine in Stage 1.

## First Market Focus

The first launch should focus on Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates.

Abu Dhabi should be treated as the first controlled pilot market. The goal is to prove that individual landlords and family property owners can manage real rental operations simply, securely, and at low cost before expanding to the rest of the UAE.

## Stage 1 Product Scope

Stage 1 targets individual landlords and family property owners who manage their own buildings, units, tenants, contracts, rent payments, expenses, and reports.

Stage 1 should support owner-managed teams such as:

- Account owner.
- Family member.
- Business manager.
- Accountant.
- Caretaker or guard.
- Trusted third party.

The account owner is the administrative user inside the workspace and should control access, roles, permissions, and team membership. This role should not be treated as proof that the user is always the legal property owner.

Tenants are records only in Stage 1. They should not have login accounts yet.

## Future Product Direction

Later stages may add:

- Property management company support.
- Multiple legal property owner clients inside one management workspace.
- Tender or quote-based maintenance workflows.
- Cleaning and property operations workflows.
- Verified rental market based only on units already managed in the system.
- Tenant portal.
- Daily or short-term rental workflows.
- Mobile applications.
- SaaS subscription billing and package upgrades.
- Aggregated and anonymized data intelligence.

## Revenue Direction

The first priority is adoption and operational trust.

Revenue should come later through:

- Individual landlord subscriptions.
- Company plans.
- Fixed package upgrades.
- Fixed module subscriptions.
- Transparent usage packages for external costs when necessary.
- Future analytics and intelligence products.

Revenue should not come from commissions or revenue share on landlord rental income, short-stay booking income, maintenance job value, or cleaning job value.

Real estate sales modules, open broker posting, and general classifieds should remain outside the product scope.
